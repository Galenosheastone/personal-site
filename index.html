About

Biochemist &
systems biologist

I'm a biochemist and systems biologist based at Montana State University. After earning my PhD in Biochemistry—building NMR-based metabolomics pipelines for wildlife conservation and translational studies—I joined Dr. Dana Rashid's lab to explore how sterile inflammation guides bone growth and fusion during vertebrate development.

Our group recently secured a W.M. Keck Foundation award to map inflammation-driven skeletogenesis across birds and mammals. My daily work spans whole-tissue transcriptomics of mouse fracture and avian tail-bud models, high-resolution µCT and confocal imaging, and integrative analytics merging RNA, imaging, and histological data in reproducible R/Python workflows.

I thrive on cross-disciplinary collaboration, transparent code, and mentoring emerging scientists in quantitative methods.

Research

Areas of focus

01

Inflammation-Mediated Skeletal Morphogenesis

Investigating how sterile inflammatory pathways—cGAS-STING, necroptosis, and DAMP signaling—drive bone fusion during development, with a focus on sacral and pygostyle fusion in birds.

02

Evolutionary Origins of Bone Fusion

Comparative studies across avian and mammalian lineages to understand the deep evolutionary mechanisms behind skeletal consolidation events, from dinosaurs to modern birds.

03

Biomarkers of Regeneration & Nutritional Stress

Applying NMR metabolomics and multivariate statistics to identify serum biomarkers in wildlife conservation (bighorn sheep) and translational disease models.

04

Computational Methods for Biological Data

Building open-source R/Python pipelines for transcriptomic analysis, interactive pathway visualization, and machine-learning classification of high-dimensional omics datasets.
